# Product Story: Notely AI

## Product Overview

Notely AI is an AI meeting assistant for professionals and small teams. Users upload meeting recordings and receive transcripts, concise summaries, action items, and shareable meeting notes.

The product is designed for people who spend a lot of time in meetings and need a faster way to capture decisions, follow-ups, and next steps.

## Target Users

- Product managers who need meeting summaries, decision logs, and action items.
- Founders and operators who run many customer, investor, and internal meetings.
- Sales and customer success teams that need call notes and follow-up summaries.
- Consultants who need structured notes from client calls.
- Students or individual users who may use the free plan but are less likely to subscribe.

## Plans

### Free Plan

The Free plan is designed for product discovery and light personal use.

Limits:

- Up to 5 meeting uploads per month.
- Maximum meeting length of 30 minutes.
- Transcript generation.
- Basic AI summary.
- 30-day history retention.
- No team workspace.
- No advanced action item extraction.
- No Slack, Notion, or Google Docs integrations.

### Pro Subscription

The Pro plan is designed for professionals and teams who use meeting intelligence repeatedly.

Benefits:

- Higher monthly transcription limits.
- Longer meeting recording support.
- Advanced AI summaries.
- Action item extraction.
- Team workspace.
- Slack, Notion, and Google Docs integrations.
- Shareable meeting summaries.
- Long-term history retention.

## Core Product Value

Notely AI creates value when a user uploads a real meeting and receives a useful AI-generated summary. The first moment of value is not account creation. It happens when the user sees a summary or action items that would have taken manual effort to write.

## Business Model

Notely AI uses a freemium subscription model. Users begin on the Free plan, hit usage or feature limits, view the paywall, and may upgrade to Pro.

The main business questions are:

- Are new users reaching the first value moment?
- Which channels bring high-quality users?
- Which product actions predict retention and subscription?
- Are Pro features increasing willingness to pay?
- Are pricing and free-limit changes improving monetization without hurting retention too much?
